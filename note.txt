<h2>Elective Courses:</h2>
<p>
  Universities often offer a range of elective courses that allow students to
  specialize in specific areas within their chosen discipline. For example:
</p>
<ul>
  <li>Artificial Intelligence</li>
  <li>Robotics</li>
  <li>Renewable Energy</li>
  <li>Machine Learning</li>
  <li>Web Development</li>
  <li>VLSI Design</li>
  <li>Bioinformatics</li>
</ul>

<h2>Laboratory and Practical Courses:</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Engineering Workshop</li>
  <li>Computer Programming Lab</li>
  <li>Electronics Lab</li>
  <li>Mechanical Design Lab</li>
  <li>Control Systems Lab</li>
</ul>

<h2>Project Work:</h2>
<p>
  B.Tech programs often require students to undertake a final-year project where
  they apply their knowledge to solve real-world engineering problems.
</p>

<h2>Soft Skills and General Education Courses:</h2>
<p>
  Some universities include courses to develop students' communication skills,
  teamwork, and general awareness. These might include subjects like Technical
  Communication, Ethics in Engineering, and Professional Development.
</p>
